About

Grand View on Moreton

Hilltop 4 bedroom beach house at Tangalooma Wild Dolphin Resort.
Sleeps 10. Maximum of 8 adults. Ideal for 2 families.
No 4WD. No problem. Catch the Tangalooma Flyer then use our 4 seater buggy.

The House. Architect designed, open plan living and a large deck with panoramic views across Moreton bay. Its north facing, hilltop location captures the sea breezes. 6 star energy rating, double glazed, insulated, eco-friendly materials and feature timber work. There is a fully equipped kitchen, BBQ, 2 family rooms, each with large TV?s and blu-ray sound systems.

The View. Brisbane to Caloundra, with the Glass House mountains and Tangalooma wrecks anchorage centre view. Sit back and watch Moreton bay, alive with boats, yachts, tankers and cruise ships. You might even catch a view of the whales during winter. Best of all are the sunsets that have inspired at least one marriage proposal on our deck.

Tangalooma Resort: The house is part of the resort and our guest may use all resort facilities (except dolphin feeding) including swimming pools. Explore the resort, take a swim or dine at the resort. The wildlife includes numerous bird species, dolphins, fish, turtles and dugongs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring?
Bed linen: fitted sheet, flat sheet, 2 pillow cases, bath towel, beach towel for each person. However, a linen hire service is available costing $17/person for the length of your stay.
How do I get there without a 4WD?
The Tangalooma Flyer departs from the Pinkenba terminal 4 times/day. Your luggage and provisions are loaded onto the boat for you. The local taxi service, providing you have booked it in advance, will transport 8 people and all luggage up to the house for a cost of $15. 
Tangalooma Flyer:
Phone: +61 7 3203 4241 
Passenger ferry that leaves from Hamilton.
Alternatively, if you are feeling energetic you may, walk up to the house, drive the buggy down to the buggy parking area (near the helicopter), and transport your own luggage to the house. The buggy is licensed to transport a maximum of 4.
How do I get there with a 4WD?
MICAT 4WD and passenger ferry departs from the port of Brisbane on a daily basis arriving at "the wrecks", approximately 1km north of the resort. Drive on, relax in the lounge, then 75 minutes later, drive off onto Moreton Island beach. 
Once on the beach, drive north 200 metres and access middle road. After 1 km, turn right and follow the road that runs behind Tangalooma resort. Enter the southern access point and locate 10 Cowry Place (top of the hill). Park in the driveway.
