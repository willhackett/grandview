THE STAY

Hilltop 4 bedroom beach house at Tangalooma Wild Dolphin Resort.
Sleeps 10. Maximum of 8 adults. Ideal for 2 families.
No 4WD. No problem. Catch the Tangalooma Flyer then use our 4 seater buggy.

The House. Architect designed, open plan living and a large deck with panoramic views across Moreton bay. Its north facing, hilltop location captures the sea breezes. 6 star energy rating, double glazed, insulated, eco-friendly materials and feature timber work. There is a fully equipped kitchen, BBQ, 2 family rooms, each with large TV?s and blu-ray sound systems.

The View. Brisbane to Caloundra, with the Glass House mountains and Tangalooma wrecks anchorage centre view. Sit back and watch Moreton bay, alive with boats, yachts, tankers and cruise ships. You might even catch a view of the whales during winter. Best of all are the sunsets that have inspired at least one marriage proposal on our deck.

Tangalooma Resort: The house is part of the resort and our guest may use all resort facilities (except dolphin feeding) including swimming pools. Explore the resort, take a swim or dine at the resort. The wildlife includes numerous bird species, dolphins, fish, turtles and dugongs.

House Facilities

Linen is available for rent on request for those living away from the Brisbane area

HOW TO GET THERE:

1.    WITHOUT A 4WD
The Tangalooma Flyer. 4 trips/day. Check-in your luggage and provisions. Board the ferry. 75 minutes later, you’re at the resort . Your luggage is delivered to the resorts taxi pick-up area. The taxi will transport you and your luggage up to the house ($15). You're there.
ELECTRIC BUGGY: For guests staying at the house without a 4WD, a 4 person buggy is available on request. Very strict safety conditions apply. Walking access to the resort is a short 10 minute downhill walk. If you have a 4WD, car parking is next to the buggy parking at the Helipad, and the buggy is not necessary for resort access or picking up guests arriving on the Tangalooma Flyer.

2.    WITH a 4WD:
MICAT 4WD Ferry. Departs from the port of Brisbane.  90 minutes and you’re on the island. The drive takes about 30 minutes to reach the house. There is parking for two 4WD’s.
